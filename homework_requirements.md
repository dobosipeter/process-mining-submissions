## Part 1: BPMN Process Modeling (01 Beadandó)

**Open:** 2026-03-02 (Monday)  
**Due:** 2026-04-11 (Saturday) 00:00

Model a (sub)system related to your thesis as a BPMN diagram, with a detailed written description of the processes and how they map onto the BPMN model. If relevant (i.e. process redesign is involved), you may create both an **as-is** and **to-be** model.

### Deliverables

1. **Textual description** (PDF or Word) -- can be submitted as part of the full thesis document.
2. **Editable BPMN diagram** -- a `.bpmn` file.

## Part 2: Process Mining Analysis

Covered in the afternoon session. Submit an analysis that includes:

1. **A clear stated goal** -- e.g. "I want to investigate the most frequent transitions" or "I want to find the busiest resource."
2. **Task description** -- what is the data, where does it come from, what does it contain.
3. **Analysis using the techniques covered in class** -- process discovery, conformance checking, comparing models, etc. (pm4py / Python).
4. **Model comparison** -- compare process models discovered with different algorithms and justify your choice.

### Data selection

- Use your own event log data if available (e.g. from your workplace or thesis).
- If you don't have your own data, download a public dataset from the **BPI Challenge** site or similar sources (linked in the slides/PPT).
